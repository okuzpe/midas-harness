# TaskPilot — Product Idea

> **Phase 0 → v1** (raw idea, preserved verbatim)
> **Phase 1 → v2** (gaps filled, pitch sharpened; see additions marked [v2])

---

## Raw idea (Phase 0 — preserved verbatim)

"I want to build a simple task manager for small teams — something like a lightweight Jira or Linear
but without all the bloat. Teams of 2–10 people. You create tasks, assign them, set a status, and
comment on them. Maybe a kanban board. No epics, no story points, no roadmap inside the tool itself.
Just tasks that are either todo, in-progress, or done."

---

## 1-line pitch

**TaskPilot** — a zero-bloat task manager for teams of 2–10 that gets out of your way.

---

## Mode

`greenfield` — new product, starting from Phase 0.

---

## [v2] Sharpened definition (Phase 1 output)

### Users

- **Primary:** Individual contributors on small product teams (2–10 people, e.g. early-stage
  startups, indie studios, small agency pods).
- **Secondary:** The single team lead who creates the workspace and invites members.

### Problem being solved

Existing tools (Jira, Asana, ClickUp) carry onboarding overhead and feature sprawl that slows down
small teams. Linear is closer but still requires project/team hierarchy that is overhead for a 2-person
startup. Teams end up in Notion or spreadsheets and lose accountability. TaskPilot gives them a real
task board without the configuration tax.

### Success metric (primary)

**Task-completion rate**: ≥ 70% of created tasks reach `done` status within 14 days of creation,
measured across pilot teams during the first 60 days post-launch.

### Non-goals (explicit)

- No epics, sprints, story-point estimation, or roadmap views inside the product.
- No real-time collaborative editing (Google-Docs-style).
- No native mobile apps in v1.
- No Gantt / timeline views.
- No SSO or enterprise auth in v1 (email + password only).
